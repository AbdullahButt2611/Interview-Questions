# Why Machine Learning Became Popular in Recent Years

## In One Line

ML algorithms are old, but two missing pieces finally arrived together: huge amounts of easy-to-get, easy-to-label data, and cheap, powerful hardware to learn from it.

<br><br>

## Quick Context

Most ML ideas, even neural networks, have been around for decades. They did not take off back then because the "fuel" and the "engine" were missing. In recent years both showed up at once, and ML went from interesting theory to something that works remarkably well.

<br><br>

## 1. Data (the fuel)

- **Before:** collecting and labelling data was slow and tedious. There was little digital data around, and humans had to hand-label most examples one by one.
- **Now:** the internet, smartphones, sensors, and apps produce massive data every second. Labelling is far easier too, thanks to crowdsourcing, ready-made public datasets, and tools that help auto-label.
- **Why it matters:** ML learns from examples, so more and better-labelled data lets models spot richer patterns and become far more accurate. A student learns more from 10,000 solved problems than from 10.

<br><br>

## 2. Hardware (the engine)

- **Before:** older computers were not powerful enough to train large models in a reasonable time. Training was either painfully slow or simply not possible.
- **Now:** GPUs (graphics chips built for gaming) turned out to be perfect for the heavy parallel math that ML needs. With GPUs, TPUs, more memory, and cheap cloud computing, training that once took months can take hours.
- **Why it matters:** faster, cheaper computing means you can train bigger models on bigger data and experiment quickly instead of waiting forever. Same recipe, but now you have a giant oven instead of a candle.

<br><br>

## Putting It Together

These two feed each other. Big data needs strong hardware to process it, and strong hardware makes big data useful. When both arrived at the same time, results jumped, and those visible wins (image recognition, recommendations, chatbots) are what made ML famous.

<br><br>

## Key Takeaway

ML did not become popular because the math suddenly changed. It became popular because the two things it always needed finally became available together: lots of easy data, and powerful, affordable hardware.
